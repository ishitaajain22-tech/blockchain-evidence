# Judges & Prosecutors Guide: Court Use of EVID-DGC

## Legal Framework & Admissibility

### Federal Rules of Evidence Compliance

**Rule 901 - Authenticating Evidence**
EVID-DGC satisfies authentication requirements through:
1. **Testimony of Witness with Knowledge** - System administrators can testify to system operation
2. **Comparison with Authenticated Specimen** - Hash comparison with blockchain records
3. **Distinctive Characteristics** - Unique cryptographic hashes serve as digital fingerprints

### Blockchain Evidence Admissibility Standards
**Technical Requirements:**
1. **Reliability**: System must be shown to be reliable
2. **Accuracy**: Evidence must be accurately recorded
3. **Completeness**: Full chain of custody must be maintained
4. **Authenticity**: Evidence must be what it purports to be

## Judicial Review Process

### Pre-Trial Motions
**Common Defense Arguments:**
1. **"Blockchain technology is unproven"**
   - Response: Cite technical standards and legal precedents
2. **"Chain of custody is incomplete"**
   - Response: Demonstrate automated custody tracking
3. **"Evidence could be tampered with"**
   - Response: Explain cryptographic impossibility

### Sample Jury Instruction - Blockchain Evidence
"Ladies and gentlemen of the jury, you will hear evidence that has been preserved using blockchain technology. Blockchain is a secure method of recording information that makes it extremely difficult to alter or tamper with records."

## Court Presentation Guidelines

### Expert Witness Guidelines
**Sample Expert Testimony:**
```
Q: Can you explain blockchain technology in simple terms?
A: Imagine a notebook that's copied to thousands of computers around the world. Every time someone writes in the notebook, all the copies are updated. If someone tries to change an old entry, the other copies would show the change is fake.
```
